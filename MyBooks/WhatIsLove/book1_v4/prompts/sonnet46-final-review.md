# Review: *What Is Love?* — Effectiveness in Communicating God's Perfect Love

## Overall Assessment

This is a serious, theologically rich, and pastorally urgent work. It succeeds in its core mission — presenting a coherent, comprehensive, and distinctly Catholic definition of love — and does so with genuine intellectual rigor, real pastoral fire, and admirable structural discipline. The definition itself ("the sacred gift of covenant binding truth, justice, mercy, and sacrifice into life-giving communion") is both defensible and generative, functioning throughout the book exactly as promised: as a diagnostic key, a catechetical framework, and a call to sainthood.

That said, there are specific areas where clarity, tone, and argumentative strength could be sharpened. Here they are in priority order.

---

## Strengths

**1. The Definition Works Beautifully**
The core definition is this book's greatest gift. It's genuinely novel in its synthesis without being arbitrary. Each word carries weight; chapters build methodically on it. The "Binding Check" sections at the end of Chapters 6–9 (showing what each pillar becomes when isolated) are especially effective.

**2. The Children's Lens Is Consistently Powerful**
Beginning each chapter with a child's experience of that facet's violation — "Mommy and Daddy are getting divorced" for covenant, "You'll never change" for mercy, "That didn't happen" for truth — is the book's most distinctive and memorable literary device. It's concrete, emotionally resonant, and theologically grounded. This continues to work throughout.

**3. The Integration of the Whole**
The book genuinely builds. By Chapter 16, the payoff of showing Christ as perfectly embodying every component feels earned, not forced. The observation in Ch. 16 that the Cross is where "the prism is removed" — all four pillars converging into one act of love — is among the book's best passages.

**4. The Practical Material Is Excellent**
Chapter 13's examination of conscience using the four pillars, Chapter 15's generational impact tables, and Chapter 16's "Concrete Next Steps" are highly usable pastoral tools. These elevate the book beyond theoretical theology.

---

## Areas Needing Attention

### 1. Tone Shift: Introduction and Chapter 1 Are Too Polemical

Problem: The author rightly says "If you're not Catholic, I invite you to journey with me anyway." But by page 3 of the Introduction, the reader has already encountered explicit condemnation of several ideological positions they may hold. This contracts the invitation before it's been fully extended.

**Recommendation:** The polemical specifics work better in Chapters 14–15, where they have argumentative context. In the Introduction, the tone risks sounding like a culture-war manifesto rather than a love letter.

### 2. The Preface's AI Disclosure Is a Distraction

**The problem:** The Preface's paragraph on AI assistance, while honest and admirable in intent, interrupts the spiritual register of the preface and introduces an awkward note of defensiveness ("fear not, this book was not 'written by AI'"). It's also the longest paragraph in the Preface.

**Recommendation:** Either cut this paragraph entirely or move it to a brief Author's Note at the end. The preface's genuine strengths — the humility about authorship, the attribution to the Holy Spirit and the Church, the personal witness of the 1988 retreat — are undercut by pivoting to AI tools. The reader who most needs reassurance about AI-assisted writing is not the target audience; the reader who would be troubled by it will be *more* troubled by the extensive discussion than by a brief note.

### 3. Chapter 11 (The Moral Argument) Is Misplaced

**The problem:** Chapter 11 interrupts the pastoral momentum at a critical juncture. After ten chapters of profound theological and practical development, the reader is ready to be tested and applied — and instead encounters a formal philosophical argument for God's existence. This is valuable material, but it feels like a detour rather than a climax.

Additionally, Chapter 11 introduces the Euthyphro dilemma and its resolution — an important philosophical discussion — but assumes a reader who is simultaneously theologically formed (having read ten chapters) and philosophically skeptical. These are somewhat different audiences.

**Recommendation:** Consider moving the core of Chapter 11 earlier — perhaps as an appendix to Chapter 3 (Sacred), or as a sidebar within it. Alternatively, reframe Chapter 11 as "How to Share This With Non-Believers" rather than a standalone philosophical argument. The bridge it provides from definition to apologetics is real, but its current placement breaks the book's arc.

### 4. The Definition's "Binding" Chapter (Ch. 5) Is the Weakest

**The problem:** "Binding" is the most abstract of the nine components, and this chapter works hardest to justify its inclusion. The structural argument (love as "form of virtues" per CCC 1827) is theologically solid, but the chapter lacks the visceral, memorable illustration that anchors the others. The Eucharist section is the chapter's high point, but it arrives late.

The "What Children Know About Binding" section — fragmented families using children as weapons — is effective but covers territory already handled in the Covenant chapter. The distinction between "covenant breaking" (Ch. 4) and "unity shattering" (Ch. 5) is real but requires careful reading to register.

**Recommendation:** Open the chapter with the Eucharist illustration rather than saving it. Or find a simpler, more visceral image for binding — a rope, a body, a musical chord — that demonstrates immediately what is lost when any strand is removed. The Woman Caught in Adultery (John 8) section is excellent; consider making that the chapter's organizing center.

### 5. Chapters 14 and 15 Have Significant Overlap

**The problem:** Both chapters apply the diagnostic matrix to counterfeits. Chapter 14 identifies what each counterfeit is missing; Chapter 15 shows what each does to children. The material is genuinely complementary, but the tables repeat much of the same content, and the chapters read as variations of the same argument rather than as distinct advances.

**Recommendation:** Consider consolidating. Chapter 14 could identify the counterfeits with brief diagnostic tables, and Chapter 15 could then apply them to children without re-litigating the diagnosis. The current structure requires the reader to hold ~15 counterfeits through two full chapters of nearly parallel analysis.


### 7. Chapter 12 (Virtues/Faith Working Through Love) Is Dense

**The problem:** The virtue matrix is a powerful analytical tool, but the chapter moves quickly from the matrix to a dense theological argument about justification, the Council of Trent, and the James/Paul debate on faith and works. These are among the most contested topics in Christian theology, and the chapter covers them in rapid succession.

The argument for Catholic soteriology is sound and well-supported, but readers who arrive at this book from Protestant traditions — whom the Preface explicitly invites — may feel that the book's ecumenical openness has given way to direct polemic by Chapter 12.

**Recommendation:** Slow down slightly on the justification section. The family analogy (the addicted son whose father "covers it up" vs. actually pursues healing) is one of the book's best arguments — give it more room to breathe.


---

## Minor Recommendations

- **Chapter 10's ending ("Will you answer?")** is effective but arrives at a "call to sainthood" climax rather early — the book has six more chapters to go. Consider softening slightly to distinguish this from the book's actual conclusion.

- **The Binding Check sections** (Chs. 6–9) are excellent but feel slightly formulaic by Ch. 9. Consider varying the format slightly in later chapters.

- **The closing prayer in Chapter 16** is genuinely beautiful and a strong finish. Well done.

- **The Further Study sections** are excellent throughout — well-curated and appropriately varied. These will serve parish discussion groups well.

- **The dedication** ("For my six sons and all the children God's Love wants to save") is right and good.

---

## Summary Judgment

*What Is Love?* is a mature, substantive, and genuinely needed work. Its core insight — that the Church's definition of love is not vague sentiment but a precise, integrated, and verifiable framework — is both true and underdeveloped in contemporary Catholic writing. The book makes this case well.

Its greatest risks are: (1) being preached primarily to the convinced rather than evangelizing the searching, and (2) allowing the culture-war framing to obscure the book's deeper pastoral gentleness, which is real and comes through clearly in the best passages. Sharpening the opening tone and tightening the structural redundancies in Chapters 14–15 would meaningfully expand its reach.

The book's theology is sound. Its pastoral heart is evident. Its definition holds. With targeted revisions, this could be genuinely important.
