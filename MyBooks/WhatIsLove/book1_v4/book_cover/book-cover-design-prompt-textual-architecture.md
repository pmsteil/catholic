# Book Cover Design Prompt — "What Is Love?"
## TEXTUAL ARCHITECTURE APPROACH
### Inspired by the Summa Theologica Infographic

**Concept:** The entire book cover image is CONSTRUCTED FROM WORDS AND CONCEPTS RELATED TO LOVE. Just as the Summa Theologica visualization creates a cathedral from theological text and teachings, this cover builds a SACRED ARCHITECTURE FROM THE TOTALITY OF CATHOLIC TEACHING ON LOVE.

---

## Core Idea

The cover should feature a central sacred image (Cross, Sacred Heart, Church, or Altar) that is NOT simply drawn, but **COMPOSED ENTIRELY OF WORDS AND THEOLOGICAL CONCEPTS** arranged to form the shapes, contours, and visual structure.

The words flow together to create:
- The outline of the cross
- The chambers and architecture of a church/cathedral
- The structure of a heart
- The rays of light
- The foundational altar
- All structural and decorative elements

This approach does several things at once:
1. **Visually demonstrates** that all teaching and all truth converge in Christ's love
2. **Honors the density and depth** of Catholic theology
3. **Creates a striking, original image** that distinguishes this from generic religious art
4. **Makes the cover itself a teaching tool** — readers can explore the words and find meaning
5. **Reflects the book's own approach** — layering Scripture, Catechism, saints, and 2,000 years of wisdom

## Visual Approach

### OPTION 1: THE CATHEDRAL APPROACH
Like the Summa infographic, arrange the words to form the architectural structure of a church or cathedral:
- Words form the walls, pillars, arches, roof, windows, doors
- Key phrases anchor major structural points (e.g., the altar, the cross, the foundation)
- The four pillars (Truth, Justice, Mercy, Sacrifice) form the load-bearing architecture
- Sacraments arranged around the perimeter or in specific sacred spaces within the architecture
- Virtues woven throughout, layering complexity
- The entire structure reads as a unified whole but contains infinite depth

### OPTION 2: THE CRUCIFORM APPROACH
Arrange words to form the shape of a CROSS at the center:
- The horizontal bar made of words about "Sacrifice," "Mercy," "Redemption"
- The vertical bar made of words about "Truth," "Justice," "Covenant," "Eternal Life"
- Radiating outward from the cross, concentric circles or expanding layers of words representing the virtues, sacraments, commandments, and Beatitudes flowing outward
- The entire composition reads as a cross with emanating theological content

### OPTION 3: THE SACRED HEART APPROACH
Arrange words to form the shape of a SACRED HEART:
- Words about the wounds, the flame, the light from within
- The three layers of tissue (Truth, Justice, Mercy) formed from layered text
- Crown of thorns formed from words about Sacrifice, Covenant, Suffering, Love
- Rays emanating outward from the heart, made of words about grace, redemption, salvation

### OPTION 4: THE MANDALA/ROSE WINDOW APPROACH
Arrange words in concentric circles around a central cross or heart:
- Innermost circle: The four pillars (Truth, Justice, Mercy, Sacrifice)
- Next rings: The sacraments, virtues, commandments
- Outer rings: Scriptural passages, Beatitudes, works of mercy
- The entire composition resembles a rose window or mandala — geometrically balanced but theologically dense

## Design Specifications

### Palette
- **Ground:** Dark wine, deep navy, or charcoal (matte finish, reverential)
- **Text colors:** Gold, ivory, pale rose, deep violet, deep crimson — varying to distinguish categories
- Use color to organize: 
  - Gold for Truth
  - Violet for Justice
  - Pale rose for Mercy
  - Deep crimson for Sacrifice
  - Additional jewel tones for sacraments and virtues

### Typography
- **Body text** (the theological content): Small, legible serif font (appropriate for infographic detail)
- **Key phrases/headings:** Slightly larger, bold, used to anchor major visual points
- **Title "What Is Love?"**: Positioned at top or integrated into the architecture, in classical serif, restrained size
- **Subtitle:** Below title if not integrated, in smaller serif
- **No author name on front cover**

### Rendering Style
- **NOT photorealistic** — this is an infographic/diagram approach
- Highly detailed line work suggesting medieval manuscript or infographic precision
- Some areas may be denser with text (like the Summa image), others lighter (negative space for visual breathing room)
- The overall effect should be that of a precious, complex theological diagram — like opening a medieval theological treatise
- Must be print-ready (high contrast, 300 DPI aesthetic)

### Composition
- **Central focal point:** A clear primary sacred image (cross, heart, church) that is immediately readable
- **Radiating complexity:** Detail increases from center outward, or is organized by architectural/geometric zones
- **Balance:** Despite the density of text, the composition should feel intentional and balanced, not chaotic
- **Negative space:** Strategic use of dark background to make text "float" and maintain legibility

## Production Notes

- **Final dimensions:** 6" × 9" (standard trade paperback)
- **Bleed:** 0.125" all sides
- **Resolution:** 300 DPI minimum
- **Color mode:** CMYK for print; RGB/sRGB for digital
- **Print finish:** Matte (reverential, not glossy)
- **Font legibility:** Ensure smallest text is legible at actual book cover size and print resolution

---

## Creative Freedom for the Designer

The designer should use the above categories as a **source palette** but make judicious choices about:
- Which specific words and phrases to include (not ALL possible words — that would be unreadable; choose the most theologically significant and visually balanced selections)
- How densely to populate different zones
- Which color corresponds to which concept
- The exact architectural or geometric structure
- How the title and subtitle integrate into the composition
- Whether to include decorative borders or keep it minimal

The goal is **theological comprehensiveness that is also visually coherent and striking**.

## Summary

This cover is not a illustration or a painting. It is a **theological diagram rendered as sacred art**. Every word matters. The structure itself teaches. The image and the content are one.

This approach honors:
- The book's own method (layering Scripture, Catechism, saints, tradition)
- The depth and comprehensiveness of Catholic teaching
- The precision and reverence of medieval theological art
- The reader's intelligence and desire for substance
- The transcendent nature of what is being transmitted: the definition of God's Perfect Love
