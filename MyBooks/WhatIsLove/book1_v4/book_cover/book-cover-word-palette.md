# Word Palette — "What Is Love?" Book Cover
## Textual Architecture Design — Complete Theological Word List

All terms, phrases, Scripture passages, and theological concepts to be used
as raw material for constructing the book cover image. The designer selects
from this palette to populate the visual architecture — not all words will
appear, but every word here belongs theologically.

---

## THE CORE DEFINITION

> *God's Perfect Love is the sacred gift of covenant —*
> *binding truth, justice, mercy and sacrifice —*
> *into life-giving communion.*

> *Deus Caritas Est* — God is Love (1 John 4:8)

## DESIGNER NOTES

- **Color assignments by pillar:** Gold = Truth, Violet = Justice, Rose-white = Mercy, Crimson = Sacrifice
- **Use the Core Definition** as a central inscription — it is the theological DNA of the entire book
- **Latin phrases** make excellent structural anchors (e.g., "Deus Caritas Est" at the center)
- **Scripture passages** work well as flowing rivers, arches, or border elements
- **Sacramental vocabulary** can populate distinct architectural zones (e.g., each nave bay = one sacrament)
- **The Beatitudes** work beautifully as curved text following arches or circular mandala rings
- **Single words** (Truth, Justice, Mercy, Sacrifice, Grace, Covenant) make powerful structural headers
- **Phrases from 1 Corinthians 13** ("Love is patient, love is kind, love never fails") are universally recognizable and anchor meaning for any reader
- **Not every word need appear** — selectivity is an art. The image should be legible at a distance and rewarding up close
