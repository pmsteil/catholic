## Frame

- **Title:** *Prudence — How to Love*
- **Position:** Between current Ch. 10 (Life-Giving Communion) and current Ch. 11 (Law Requires a Lawgiver → becomes Ch. 12). The practical hinge after the full definition (Chs. 1–10).
- **Length target:** ~210 lines (matching `chapter_12.md`). Framework, not exhaustion — hands the rest to Book 2.
- **Thesis:** Knowing the four facets of love is not the same as knowing how to love *this* person, in *this* moment. Prudence is the virtue that sees the one integrated act love requires here — and the father of the Prodigal Son is its icon.

### Spine (LOCKED)

One master lie unifies every case study:

> **"To love is to affirm what someone wants and feels; truth, limits, and cost are the enemies of love."**

The culture allows only two responses — **total affirmation (counterfeit mercy)** or **hateful rejection (counterfeit truth)**. Perfect Love's **third way** is the **father of Luke 15:11–32**, recurring as the chapter's icon. The deeper lie beneath, which Ch. 12 (Lawgiver) then answers: *"I am my own. There is no Author of my life to whom I owe the truth of what I am."*

### Guardrail (`AGENTS.md` "TJMS as one, not four")

This is the ONE chapter where binding / weaving / integrating language is fully correct — it describes how *we* (finite creatures lacking divine simplicity) love, **not** what God *is*. Keep that distinction explicit so it harmonizes with the full-book pass.

## Section-by-Section

### 1. The Problem of Application (hook)
- The gap: you can recite the four pillars and *still* freeze when the friend confesses, the child calls, the parent asks to die.
- Anchors: *James 1:22* ("be doers of the word, and not hearers only"); *CCC 25*; callback to Ch. 9's open hand ("the hand opens by *doing*").
- Tease the case studies.

### 2. Prudence: The Cardinal Virtue of Application
- Show before naming: the person who knows the good *and* does it at the right moment, in the right way. Then attach the term.
- Aquinas: *recta ratio agibilium* ("right reason in acting"); *CCC 1806*; *auriga virtutum* ("the charioteer of the virtues").
- Kill misreadings: prudence is not timidity, fence-sitting, or worldly caution — it is **the intelligence of love.**

### 3. The Prodigal Son: The Icon of How to Love (spine section)
Walk Luke 15:11–32 as all four pillars in one story:
- **Free will** — he lets the son go (the Free Will Principle in one gesture).
- **Truth** — even at the feast: "this my son was dead… was lost." No pretending the far country didn't happen.
- **Justice** — robe, ring, sandals: true sonship/dignity restored (not a sentimental pass).
- **Mercy** — "while he was yet at a distance, his father saw him" (watched the road every day), then *ran*.
- **Sacrifice** — the fatted calf, the cost gladly paid; communion restored.

The two counterfeits *in the text*:
- **Mercy without truth** = a party while the son is still in the far country (the father never does this — never sends the calf into the pigsty to validate the son).
- **Truth without mercy** = the **elder brother** (technically correct, loving no one).

Land the recurring question: *Which son are you looking at — and will you be the father, or one of the counterfeits?*

### 4. The Four Pillars Only Seem to Conflict
- When truth seems to pull against mercy (etc.), we are **misreading one pillar**. Properly seen, they converge on one act (anthropological binding; callback to Ch. 5).
- Quick reversals from the Ch. 5 separation list (truth-without-mercy → stoning the adulteress; mercy-without-truth → enabling; etc.).

### 5. The Order of Love (*Ordo Amoris*) — MOVED from Ch. 9
- Relocate `chapter_09.md` L288–302 (`## The Hierarchy of Sacrifice`), reframed as a four-pillars-application doctrine (not sacrifice-specific). Leave a one-line pointer in Ch. 9.
- Order: **God → one's own soul → spouse/children → extended family/community → neighbor/stranger → enemy.** Ordering of *obligation*, not affection.
- Finitude point (author's): *we are not God.* His truth/justice/mercy/sacrifice are infinite; ours are finite — one body, one life, one measure of time. Prudence is how finite love is rightly *spent*, not exhausted on disordered fronts. (*ST* II-II q.26; "we can never sin to help another.")

### 6. Case Studies: Discernment in Action (LOCKED)

Each case = the same master lie, a different son, a different pillar attacked.

Pattern for each: *the lie → which son → apparent conflict → the two counterfeits (which pillar each drops) → the father's integrated act → the* ordo amoris *note.*

**Case 1 — The adult child who demands affirmation** (transgender identity / same-sex partner / cohabiting) → attacks **Truth**.
- Counterfeits: affirm to keep the relationship (party in the far country) // cut them off (elder brother).
- Father's act: never lie, never affirm the sin, never close the door, watch the road. **Love is not approval.**
- *Ordo amoris:* a primary covenant bond (high claim), but you cannot sacrifice your own soul (the truth) to keep their affection.

**Case 2 — Euthanasia / "death with dignity"** (aging parent asks for help dying; pressure to end a "life not worth living" — disabled newborn, dementia) → attacks **Justice**.
- Lie: dignity = utility.
- Counterfeits: "killing is the merciful thing" (counterfeit mercy) // vitalism that idolizes machines (failure of ordinary vs. extraordinary means).
- Father's act: accompany suffering without killing — real pain relief, presence, the dignity of the person who *is* not who *does* (Ch. 9: "covenant says I give because you *are*").
- *Ordo amoris:* the author's "parent in the final years" case. Current frontier (Canada MAID, Benelux).

**Case 3 — The therapeutic divorce** ("I'm just not happy anymore"; valid, wearying, *non-abusive* marriage) → attacks **Sacrifice**.
- Counterfeits: "you deserve to be happy, follow your heart" // cold "you made your bed, suffer in it."
- Father's act: the costly truth about covenant *inside* their real pain; the paradox of Matt 16:24–25 (lose your life to find it); help carry it.
- Note: stay distinct from **abuse** (Ch. 9 owns that — "divorce becomes an option before sacrifice does"). The hard version is the valid, joyless-but-not-abusive marriage.

**Case 4 — The repentant grave sinner** (post-abortive mother / the abortion doctor who comes to his senses) → **the Mercy climax**.
- The two despairs: "some things are unforgivable" // "it was nothing, don't feel guilty."
- Father's act: mercy flowing *through* a justice already paid on the Cross (Ch. 9) — name the truth (it was killing), proclaim the greater truth (no sin beyond God's mercy — *CCC 982*, the good thief, Luke 23:42–43), point to real penance + healing (Confession, Project Rachel / Rachel's Vineyard, a changed life), accompany at cost.
- **The "does it hold for the WORST?" case — where the framework shines.** Bridges back to the Cross (Ch. 9) and forward to Sin (Ch. 14).

**Rapid-fire** (1–2 sentences each, to show prudence works fast in ordinary moments):
- The **homeless man at the stoplight** (sentimental mercy vs. his actual good + our finitude).
- The **murky request from a workplace superior** (truth/justice vs. self-interest disguised as loyalty).

### 7. The Formation of Prudence (the chapter's Practical Application)
Built over a lifetime, never alone:
- (a) **conscience formation** — Scripture, Tradition, Magisterium (*CCC 1783–1785*)
- (b) the **Sacraments**, esp. Confession + Eucharist
- (c) **prayer** — examen, *lectio divina*, asking the Spirit
- (d) **spiritual direction**
- (e) the **saints** as lived case studies
- (f) the **Church community** — we do not discern in isolation

### 8. The Holy Spirit's Gift of Counsel (*Consilium*)
- One of the seven gifts — *Isaiah 11:2*. Brief Ignatian discernment of spirits (consolation vs. desolation; distinguishing the Spirit's voice from our own desires). The supernatural lift of prudence.

### 9. When Prudence Fails
- We will misread, act on partial information, let passion override judgment. Not defeat — the occasion for Confession and the humility that deepens prudence. Hand-off to Sin (new Ch. 14).

### 10. Toward Book 2 / Bridge to Ch. 12
- Prudence applied to every situation is a lifetime's work — *Book 2: Practicing Perfect Love* carries it.
- Bridge: having seen *how* love is applied, we ask what grounds the moral order we've been discerning — a Law this real requires a **Lawgiver** (Ch. 12).
