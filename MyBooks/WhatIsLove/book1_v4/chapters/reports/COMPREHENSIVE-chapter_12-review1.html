🔴 HIGH Lines 89-92 (after 'Even faith requires sacrifice to be authentic—faith without love is dead.')
Missing the Protestant counter-argument on faith and works. The chapter quotes Trent's anathema on 'faith alone' but does not address the Protestant claim that adding works to faith undermines grace (sola gratia). This leaves a significant apologetic gap.
Suggested: Add a paragraph addressing this: 'Some object that requiring works alongside faith makes salvation dependent on human effort rather than grace. But this misunderstands the Catholic teaching. The works that justify are not our own—they are the fruit of grace working in us. As St. Paul writes, "For by grace you have been saved through faith; and this is not your own doing, it is the gift of God—not because of works, lest any man should boast" (Ephesians 2:8-9). The very next verse completes the thought: "For we are his workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good works, which God prepared beforehand, that we should walk in them" (Ephesians 2:10). Grace produces works; works do not produce grace. We cooperate with what God initiates.'
